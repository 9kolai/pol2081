Vasilopoulou, S., & Wagner, M. (2017). Fear, anger and enthusiasm about the European Union: Effects of emotional reactions on public preferences towards European integration.

In groups of 3-4 people, choose one of the three hypotheses from the paper to work on

  • H1: Anxious citizens are more likely to favour renegotiation than angry citizens (1a), and angry citizens are more likely to favour leaving the EU than anxious citizens (1b), while enthusiastic citizens are less likely to favour renegotiation or leaving the EU than anxious or angry citizens (1c).
  • H2: The attitudes of angry and enthusiastic citizens towards the EU are less conditional on utilitarian considerations concerning EU integration than those of anxious citizens (2a), and the attitudes of angry and enthusiastic citizens towards the EU are more conditional on affect-based considerations concerning EU integration than those of anxious citizens (2b).
  • H3: Angry and enthusiastic citizensâ€™ opinion on European integration is likely to be more uniform across policy areas than that of anxious citizens.

Group work 1 - In groups, discuss the following about your hypothesis:

  • What does the hypothesis claim in your own words?
  • What is the dependent variable, independent variable, and unit of analysis?
  • How do they define the concepts of the dependent and independent variable?
  • How do they measure these concepts?
  • Do you think this is a good hypothesis?

Group work 2 - In groups, discuss the following about your hypothesis:

  • How do they test the hypothesis?
  • How do they statistical significance?
  • What is their conclusion?
  • Do you find the overall argument convincing?
