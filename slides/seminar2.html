Hobolt & de Vries (2016). Public Support for European Integration

Defining public support for European integration

  • Regime support:
    "Regime support denotes support for the constitutional settlement of the European Union as laid down in the various treaties, including support for membership of the Union."
    • Support for EU membership (percentage of people who view their country’s membership in the European Union as a “good thing”)
  • Policy support
    "Policy support refers to support for the content of collective decisions and actions taken by EU actors."
    • Preference for speeding up integration within the EU & support for euro

Explaining public opinion towards European integration

  • Utilitarian approach
    “...European trade liberalization will favor citizens with higher levels of income and human capital (education and occupational skills) and (…) as a consequence such individuals will be more supportive of European integration.” (p. 420)
  • Identity approach
    “...individuals’ attachment to their nation and their perceptions of people from other cultures influence their attitudes toward European integration.” (p. 421)
  • Cue-taking
    “...citizens rely on proxies or cues to overcome their information shortfalls.” (p. 422)
  • Benchmarking approach
    “...domestic political institutions are a benchmark for people’s views on European integration.” (p. 422)
